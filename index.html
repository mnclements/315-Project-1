    Howdy! My name is Nicole Clements and I am a junior computing major at Texas A&M University in College Station, TX. I grew up in Plano, TX and went to high school at the Ursuline Academy of Dallas. More details about my education, work experience, and on-campus activities can be found under the "Qualifications" tab. I developed a strong interest in the computer science industry after taking a few different programming classes offered by my high school. This led me to apply to the College of Engineering at Texas A&M University in hopes of developing the knowledge necessary to succeed in a career in a computer science field. Samples of some of the work that I have done in my classes at A&M over the past few years can be found under the “Technical Portfolio” tab. Outside of class, I have also gotten the opportunity to join a few different on-campus organizations and spend time giving back to the local community in Bryan-College Station. More details about my experiences with giving back to my community can be found under the “Service” tab.

    Outside of class and my on-campus involvements, I spend my free time listening to music, going to sporting events, spending time with my friends or family, and traveling. I have an especially strong passion for travel and have been to eight different countries so far. Out of all the places that I have been, my favorite destination has been Australia. I was very fortunate to travel to Toowoomba, Australia on an educational and cultural delegation in high school. We visited our sister-school there and I was able to stay in the home of one of their students for two weeks. I learned so much on this trip and still keep in touch with my host-sister to this day. Through traveling, I have developed a global perspective and become comfortable with taking risks. I am so grateful for the travel experiences that I have had thus far and am very excited to continue exploring the world in the future. I hope to combine my computing knowledge and passion for travel by pursuing a career in the travel industry one day.
